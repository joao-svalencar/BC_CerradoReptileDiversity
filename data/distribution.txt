I checked the species listed in the SALVE system and compared the list with that provided in the Reptile Database for reptile species occurring in Brazil. 

Three species included in SALVE are not regarded as occurring in Brazil according to the Reptile Database:

- Apostolepis ambiniger: removed from our analyses following Entiauspe-Neto et al (2021)
- Philodryas patagoniensis: removed from our analyses following Di Pietro et al. (2026)

- Neusticurus medemi: kept in our analyses following Marques-Souza et al (2018)
- Plica plica: kept in our analyses following Ribeiro-Junior and Amaral (2016)

Species in the list of RDB but not included:

"Anolis porcatus", "Anolis sagrei", "Bachia alleni", "Gekko gecko", "Gonatodes concinnatus", "Hemidactylus mabouia", "Leiosaurus paronae", "Lepidodactylus lugubris",
"Ophiodes vertebralis", "Pantherophis guttatus", "Pelodiscus sinensis", "Phalotris spegazzinii", "Plica kathleenae", "Trachemys scripta", "Trachylepis maculata",
"Tropidurus guarani", "Tropidurus spinulosus", "Urotheca multilineata"

Most are introduced species or does not have proof of occurrence in the Brazilian territory.

Species of Testudines discarded from occurence in the Cerrado even though SALVE consider them as present:

"Caretta caretta", "Chelonia mydas", "Dermochelys coriacea", "Eretmochelys imbricata", "Lepidochelys olivacea", "Platemys platycephala"

Most are marine species or do not have records in the Cerrado

Species with marginal records in the Cerrado not considered to occur in the Biome in our analyses:


"Apostolepis nigrolineata": one marginal record with the bulk of records in the Amazon
"Copeoglossum arajara": one marginal record with the bulk of records in the Caatinga
"Helicops nentur": one marginal record with the bulk of records in the Atlantic Forest
"Platemys platycephala": one marginal record in Mato Grosso
"Tropidurus sertanejo": very restricted range within the Caatinga but records fall in the Cerrado shapefile. Authors describe locations as Caatinga only.
"Zonateris lanei": three very marginal records in contact with the Pantanal fall within the Cerrado shapefile.

Species not previously considered to occur in the Cerrado (according to SALVE), but with records within the biome:

"Ameivula pyrrhogularis": two records in the Cerrado. Min distance ~50km, Max distance ~110km from the nearest border with Caatinga
"Amphisbaena darwinii": three records in the Cerrado. One in Paraná and two in São Paulo. 
"Colobosauroides cearensis": two records ~50km away from the border with the Caatinga in northern Piauí
"Micrurus carvalhoi": plenty of records within the biome.
"Oxyrhopus melanogenys": five records within the Cerrado near the Amazonian border. Min distance 20km, further records ~100km away from the border

Records of Chironius xaraes and C. laurenti were extracted from Figure 9 of Sudré et al. (2026) using QGIS georeferencer

Testudines dataset were merged with the records extracted from TTWG 2025 (see R/D_sampleTestudinesTTWG.R).
